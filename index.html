Welcome to the Kim Lab @ UArizona!

The core of our research interest lies at the intersection of experimental condensed matter physics and device physics. We design, manipulate, and probe low-dimensional quantum materials and heterostructures to uncover fundamental light-matter interactions and emergent exotic states of matter in condensed matter systems. Our experimental approaches include a combination of state-of-the-art device fabrication and characterization approaches—ranging from robotic nano-manufacturing and assembly to reconfigurable device architectures to multi-modal nano-imaging. We also put a strong emphasis in translating fundamental developments made in quantum materials into novel functionalities for a new generation of electronic, photonic and quantum technologies.

Please browse our Research page to learn more about the research opportunities we offer, and't hesitate to reach out if you have any questions.
